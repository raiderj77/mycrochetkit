Top Independent Crochet Creators to Follow in 2026

Published: January 1, 2026

Quick Answer: The crochet community is thriving thanks to independent creators who share free tutorials, creative inspiration and genuine camaraderie. In this article, we introduce five of the biggest names—Jayda InStitches, The Crochet Crowd, TL Yarn Crafts, Hooked by Robin and Bag-O-Day Crochet—who collectively reach millions of crocheters worldwide. None of them are affiliated with crochet apps; they’re independent educators whose unique perspectives have shaped modern crochet culture.

Why Celebrate Independent Crochet Creators?

In an era where every craft seems to have an app, it’s refreshing to find creators who focus purely on sharing knowledge and building community. Independent crochet instructors spend years refining their skills and producing tutorials, patterns and livestreams without being tied to any particular platform or sponsor. Their openness makes their advice universally accessible, whether you count your stitches with a digital app or prefer an analogue clicker.

Featuring these educators on our blog not only shines a light on their hard work but also introduces our readers to new voices. As you explore each creator below, consider subscribing to their channels and joining their online communities. Their creativity and authenticity are what make the crochet world so rich—and why we’re proud to celebrate them.

Meet the Top 5 Crochet Creators

1. Jayda InStitches

Jayda InStitches (Jade Gauthier-Boutin) joined YouTube in 2014 and quickly built one of the most welcoming crochet communities online. She’s best known for her easy-to-follow tutorials, engaging livestreams and cheerful personality. With over 660 thousand subscribers and more than 72 million cumulative views, Jayda covers everything from beginner basics to advanced stitch patterns. Viewers rave about her popular “How To Crochet a Granny Square” video, which has racked up millions of views.

  • Content focus: Detailed tutorials, seasonal projects, live Q&A sessions and vlogs that make you feel like part of the family.
  • Why she matters: Jayda’s warm teaching style demystifies crochet for beginners and inspires experienced crafters to try new techniques. She supports the crochet community with free patterns and charitable projects.

Visit Jayda's Channel →

2. The Crochet Crowd

Hosted by Michael Sellick (aka Mikey) and based in Nova Scotia, The Crochet Crowd offers a treasure trove of free tutorials and stitch-along series. Mikey joined YouTube in 2008 and has grown the channel to 1.27 million subscribers with over 212 million video views. The channel appeals to crocheters of all ages with both right- and left-handed tutorials and lively stitch-along projects that foster a sense of community.

  • Content focus: Comprehensive tutorials, charity projects and organized stitch-along events that bring crocheters together.
  • Why they matter: Mikey’s emphasis on inclusivity (including left-handed versions) and his generous supply of free patterns have helped thousands learn crochet without spending a cent.

Visit The Crochet Crowd →

3. TL Yarn Crafts

TL Yarn Crafts is the brainchild of Toni, who launched her channel in 2015 to share both traditional and Tunisian crochet techniques. She now has over 827 thousand subscribers with 57.79 million views. Toni’s tutorials range from beginner friendly to advanced and often include challenging projects like her “24-Hour Chunky Crochet Sweater Challenge”. Her enthusiasm for yarn reviews and unboxings keeps viewers up-to-date on the latest supplies.

  • Content focus: Step-by-step tutorials, Tunisian crochet workshops, time-based challenges and yarn/product reviews.
  • Why she matters: Toni brings a modern, polished edge to crochet content and champions underrepresented techniques like Tunisian crochet. Her approachable teaching style invites viewers to expand their skills.

Visit Toni's Channel →

4. Hooked by Robin

Robin, better known as Hooked by Robin, creates quick yet comprehensive tutorials from her home in North Devon, England. Since joining YouTube in 2014, she has amassed about 878 thousand subscribers. Her channel covers a range of projects—from amigurumi creatures to festive ornaments—and her “Crochet a Snowflake in JUST 15 MINUTES” video alone has drawn millions of views.

  • Content focus: Beginner-to-advanced tutorials, seasonal decorations and charming amigurumi patterns.
  • Why she matters: Robin’s concise teaching style and focus on quick projects make crochet accessible to busy crafters. Her festive designs inspire creativity year-round.

Visit Robin's Channel →

5. Bag-O-Day Crochet

Crystal’s channel, Bag-O-Day Crochet, has become a go-to resource for diverse crochet projects since she joined YouTube in 2014. She boasts over 1.05 million subscribers and produces videos on everything from baby dresses to blankets. Her tutorials highlight unique stitches like the Puff Star Stitch and Crocodile Stitch and are updated frequently.

  • Content focus: A wide variety of garments and accessories, unique stitch techniques and regular video updates.
  • Why she matters: Crystal’s versatility means crocheters of all skill levels can find inspiration. Her dedication to teaching unusual stitches helps viewers broaden their skills.

Visit Crystal's Channel →

How We Chose These Creators

Our goal was to spotlight independent artists who influence the crochet community without being tied to any app or sponsorship. We looked for creators who:

  • Have built large, active followings on YouTube (hundreds of thousands of subscribers).
  • Consistently share free tutorials, patterns and tips that empower their viewers.
  • Represent different regions and styles within the crochet world.
  • Stay unaffiliated with commercial apps so they can speak freely about tools and techniques.

Frequently Asked Questions About Independent Crochet Creators

Why not feature creators with app partnerships?

Creators sponsored by or affiliated with apps often have contractual obligations that influence which tools they promote. Independent creators can recommend whatever techniques, gadgets or apps they genuinely love. This freedom fosters more balanced discussions and helps you discover what works best for your personal workflow.

How can I support these creators?

Subscribe to their channels, like and comment on their videos, share their tutorials and consider purchasing their patterns or merchandise. Many also accept Patreon pledges or offer memberships that provide exclusive content. Your engagement helps them continue producing free resources.

Can I collaborate with them?

Absolutely—many of these creators partner with fellow artists, indie yarn dyers and small businesses. Reach out via their websites or social media channels and propose collaboration ideas, such as hosting a stitch-along or designing a pattern together. Be sure to respect their time and craft when proposing projects.
